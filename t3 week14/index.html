Digital Materialities Brief

Inspiration

I want to create a visual experience that captures the fleeting beauty and chaotic energy of a cosmic storm. Using Shadertoy, my goal is to design an interactive shader that combines dynamic, layered particle effects with fluid motion, creating the sensation of standing at the edge of a galactic tempest. The visuals will convey a sense of awe and mystery, as swirling energy fields, cascading light waves, and sudden bursts of radiant lightning simulate the unpredictable and raw power of the universe. This piece aims to evoke the viewer's imagination, transporting them into a boundless, otherworldly space where chaos and beauty coexist.

3. Starlight Shader and Link

Starlight series source code, please click here .

Shader 1 creates a dynamic, rotating ripple effect centered on the screen. It calculates the distance (length()) and angle (atan()) from each pixel to the center, then uses a sine wave (sin()) to generate ripples that change over time (iTime). The colors dynamically shift based on the angle and ripple intensity.

Shader 2 introduces a radial gradient (mix()) and a central glow (exp()), while enhancing the ripple effect by tying it to the radial distance (radius), adding more dynamic color changes based on angle and distance.

Shader 3 introduces new functions for added complexity: `noise()` to generate randomness, `dynamicGrid()` for a moving grid effect, and `polygonEdge()` to detect and render dynamic polygon edges.

Adds radial distortion with dynamic scaling, a rotation matrix for coordinate rotation, and a kaleidoscope effect using angle mirroring. It also replaces the grid and polygon edges from Shader 3 with a checkerboard texture, creating symmetrical, dynamic patterns.

Adds concentric rings and polygon edges to the kaleidoscope effect, using `sin()` and `step()` functions. These new geometric shapes are blended with dynamic colors, creating more intricate and complex patterns compared to Shader 2.

Introduces two new functions: `blackHole()` for creating a central distortion effect by modifying UV coordinates with a strength-based transformation, and `dynamicGrid()` for generating a moving, pulsing grid pattern using `fract()`, `step()`, and trigonometric functions like `sin()` and `cos()`.

It replaces the black hole distortion with new effects: a rotating grid, concentric ripples, a kaleidoscope effect, and dynamic polygons, adding symmetrical and geometric patterns for a more structured and visually dynamic scene.

Shader 8 adds two new distortion functions: `waveDistortion()`, which introduces horizontal and vertical wave-like deformations, and `radialDistortion()`, which applies a twisting effect based on radial distance.
